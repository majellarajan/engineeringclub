Engineering and Architecture Club

Empowering students in engineering and architecture

  • Get Started

Who

We are a group of highschools interested in expanding engineering to all.

What

The club engages in hands-on projects, competitions, and workshops to develop practical engineering skills.

Where

The club meets in Mr.Caneles room!

When

Meetings are held every Thursday after school from 3:30 to 5:00 PM.

Why

The club aims to foster interest in engineering careers and provide valuable experience in problem-solving and teamwork

How

Students can join by attending a meeting and signing up with the club advisor.

Fun Facts

STEM Major Growth

In the U.S., the number of STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) graduates has increased by over 50% in the last decade, reflecting the growing demand for technical expertise.

Women in Engineering

Women now make up about 30% of the engineering workforce in the U.S., a significant increase from the 14% reported in 2000, demonstrating progress in gender diversity in engineering.

Engineering Innovations

More than 1,000 patents are filed daily in the United States, showcasing the rapid pace of innovation in engineering and technology fields.

AI and Machine Learning

Machine learning algorithms, a subset of artificial intelligence, can now outperform humans in various tasks, such as image recognition and language translation, highlighting the transformative power of technology.

Engineering and Space

The Mars Rover missions, including Curiosity and Perseverance, have provided more detailed information about Mars than ever before, demonstrating the engineering feats required to explore other planets.

Innovative Bridges

The Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco, completed in 1937, was the longest and tallest suspension bridge in the world at the time of its completion, with a main span of 1,280 meters (4,200 feet).

Engineering Marvels

The Great Wall of China, stretching over 13,000 miles, is one of the most impressive feats of engineering and construction in history, built to protect Chinese states from invasions and raids.

Global Internet

As of 2024, over 5.5 billion people around the world have access to the internet, transforming how we communicate, work, and access information globally.
